GLP-1 side effects are real, common, and well-documented.

These aren't rare occurrences. They affect a significant proportion of people who start Ozempic, Wegovy or semaglutide — often within the first few weeks.

Clinical studies show VLCKD delivers comparable or superior fat loss to GLP-1 medications — with one critical difference: it preserves lean muscle mass throughout. GLP-1 users typically lose 25–35% of total weight loss as muscle, while VLCKD patients maintain muscle. The average ProGen patient loses 10 kg in their first month under medical supervision.
Will I regain weight after the ProGen programme like I would after stopping Ozempic? +
This is the core difference. GLP-1 works by suppressing appetite — when you stop, appetite returns and weight comes back. ProGen's 5-phase protocol includes metabolic re-education (Phase 4) and long-term maintenance (Phase 5), specifically designed to prevent rebound by changing how your body and metabolism respond to food. Yes. Nausea affects 30–44% of Ozempic users. Vomiting affects 24%. Diarrhoea affects 30%. "Ozempic face" — skin sagging due to rapid fat loss without muscle preservation — is widely reported. Long-term, the most significant concern is muscle loss: GLP-1 suppresses appetite without distinguishing between fat and muscle catabolism, leaving users with worse body composition than before.
