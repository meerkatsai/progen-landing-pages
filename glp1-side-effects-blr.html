02 — SEVEN THINGS TO KNOW BEFORE YOU INJECT

The clinical facts no one is explaining to you.

Your doctor has 8 minutes per appointment. Here is what the published research shows — clearly.

01

GLP-1 is not the miracle it looks like on social media

Clinical trials show GLP-1 drugs like Ozempic and Wegovy produce 10–15 kg of weight loss in 12–18 months — roughly the same as a standard hypocaloric diet with no medication. The dramatic results you see promoted online are outliers, not averages. Social media rewards spectacular individual stories. Science measures population averages.

0.5 kg average loss per month in clinical trials. That is less than the 1 kg/month a standard dietary programme achieves.
02

The first few kilograms are mostly water, not fat

When GLP-1 reduces your appetite, your carbohydrate intake drops sharply. This depletes glycogen — the form of sugar stored in your muscles and liver. Glycogen holds approximately 3–4 g of water per gram. When it empties, you shed water weight fast. The scale moves dramatically in the first 2–3 weeks. Most of that is fluid, not fat. It will return when you stop the drug.

3–5 kg of early weight loss is typically glycogen + water depletion. Real fat loss takes longer to appear on GLP-1.
03

You are losing muscle — not just fat

This is the one nobody talks about. When you lose weight by suppressing appetite, your body does not neatly burn pure fat. Research shows that 25–40% of weight lost on GLP-1 is lean mass — your muscle tissue. Losing muscle slows your metabolism permanently. It makes your body weaker. And it makes regaining fat much easier once the drug stops. The clinical term for this outcome is sarcopenic obesity — you look thinner but your metabolic health is worse.

25–40% of GLP-1 weight loss is muscle, not fat. Compare: a properly supervised VLCKD preserves up to 95% lean mass.
04

When you stop — the weight comes back. Fast.

This is the most important fact the GLP-1 ads do not show you. A 2026 systematic review in the British Medical Journal — covering over 9,000 patients — found that after stopping GLP-1 treatment, people regain weight at a rate of 0.4–0.8 kg every single month. Within 1.5 to 1.7 years, most patients return to their original weight. The drug suppressed appetite. It did not change metabolism. It did not build new habits. When it stops, nothing has changed except the number on your scale — temporarily.

1.5–1.7 years to return to baseline weight after stopping GLP-1. Source: BMJ 2026 meta-analysis, 9,000+ patients.
05

Your health improvements also reverse

One of GLP-1's selling points is that it improves cardiometabolic markers — blood pressure comes down, cholesterol improves, blood sugar stabilises. But the same 2026 BMJ review found that these gains also reverse after stopping treatment, within approximately 1.4 years. Blood pressure returns to pre-treatment levels. Cholesterol and blood sugar follow. The health benefit was real — but it was entirely contingent on never stopping the injections.

~1.4 years for cardiometabolic benefits (BP, cholesterol, blood glucose) to fully reverse after stopping GLP-1.
06

GLP-1 does not fix why you gained weight

Obesity is not caused by insufficient semaglutide levels. It is driven by metabolic dysfunction, hormonal imbalance, emotional eating patterns, poor lifestyle architecture, and in many cases — undiagnosed conditions like insulin resistance, PCOS, or hypothyroidism. GLP-1 suppresses appetite. It does not address any of these root causes. When you stop, the same underlying conditions are still there — and they will drive the same eating patterns and the same weight regain. The cycle begins again.

07

It could cost you ₹15,000–25,000 every month. For life.

Ozempic and Wegovy are currently priced at ₹15,000–25,000 per month in India. Since stopping the drug means regaining all the weight, anyone seeking permanent results is effectively committing to a lifetime of monthly injections. That is ₹1.8 lakh to ₹3 lakh every year — forever. A medically supervised programme that addresses root causes, preserves muscle, and delivers permanent results costs a fraction of that — as a one-time investment with no ongoing dependency.

₹3L/year is the upper cost of lifetime Ozempic use in India — for a result that reverses 1.5 years after stopping.
