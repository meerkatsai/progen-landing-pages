01 The marketing gap

The clinical average weight loss is only 0.5 kg a month

Social media rewards the spectacular. Clinical science measures averages. Across 9,000+ patients in large-scale trials, GLP-1 drugs deliver 0.5–1 kg of fat loss per month — the same result as following a structured diet with zero medication. Nobody posts their month 4 plateau.

0.5–1 kg
per month — identical to a standard diet, with the added cost of weekly injections, daily nausea, and ₹25,000/month.
02 The water weight trick

You mostly lose water and not fat

GLP-1 suppresses your appetite, cutting food intake dramatically. As calories drop, your body burns through its glycogen stores — the emergency sugar reserve held in muscles and liver. Glycogen binds 3–4 grams of water per gram. When it empties, several kilograms of water flush out within 2 weeks. The scale drops fast. Actual fat loss has barely started. When the drug stops, glycogen refills — and the water returns.

3–5 kg
of early weight loss is glycogen and water, not fat. Real fat loss starts only after this initial flush.
03 The silent consequence

You risk more muscle loss

This is the fact GLP-1 ads never show. When appetite is suppressed and calories drop severely, your body doesn't burn pure fat — it breaks down muscle too. Research consistently shows 25–40% of weight lost on GLP-1 is lean muscle mass. Less muscle means a permanently slower metabolism. You weigh less, but your body has more fat relative to muscle — which makes regaining weight almost inevitable once the drug stops.

25–40%
of GLP-1 weight loss is lean muscle, not fat. A supervised VLCKD protocol preserves up to 95% lean mass — a fundamentally different outcome.
04 The rebound nobody shows

If you stop the injection, you could gain back all the weight

The 2026 British Medical Journal meta-analysis — the most comprehensive study of its kind, covering over 9,000 patients — found that after stopping GLP-1 treatment, people regain weight at 0.4–0.8 kg every single month. The average patient returns to their original weight within 1.5 to 1.7 years. Not some patients. The average patient. The drug suppressed hunger. It did nothing to your metabolism, your habits, or the root causes of weight gain. When the drug stops, everything resumes exactly as before.

1.5–1.7 yrs
to return to original weight after stopping GLP-1. Source: West S et al., BMJ 2026 systematic review and meta-analysis.
05 The health mirage

Once you stop GLP-1, all the good things stop too

GLP-1 drugs genuinely improve cardiometabolic markers while you take them — blood pressure drops, cholesterol improves, blood sugar stabilises. This is real. But the same BMJ 2026 review found that every one of these improvements also reverses after stopping treatment, within approximately 1.4 years. Your underlying health didn't change. The drug was doing the work. When it stops, your biology goes back to exactly where it was.

~1.4 yrs
for blood pressure, cholesterol, and blood sugar improvements to fully reverse after stopping GLP-1 medication.
06 The root cause problem

It never fixes the root cause of weight gain

Obesity is not caused by insufficient semaglutide. It is driven by metabolic dysfunction, hormonal imbalance, insulin resistance, PCOS, thyroid issues, and years of accumulated lifestyle patterns. GLP-1 suppresses appetite. It addresses none of these. When the drug stops, every one of those root causes is exactly where you left it — ready to drive the same weight gain again. You haven't been treated. You've been suppressed.

It's expensive (₹25,000/mo) and results reverse if you stop

Ozempic and Wegovy are priced at ₹15,000–25,000 per month in India. Since stopping the drug means regaining all the weight, anyone seeking permanent results is effectively committing to injections for life. That's ₹1.8 lakh to ₹3 lakh per year — indefinitely.
